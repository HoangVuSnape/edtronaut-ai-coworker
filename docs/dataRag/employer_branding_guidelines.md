# Employer Branding & Internal Communications Guidelines

## Tone and Voice
All internal communications regarding HR processes, training, and talent development must reflect the prestigious, forward-thinking nature of the Gucci Group. 

- **Professional yet Accessible**: Avoid overly corporate jargon. Use inspiring language that connects daily tasks to the broader world of luxury fashion.
- **Brand Neutrality**: When addressing the entire Group, remain neutral. Avoid using terminology or stylistic choices that favor one specific brand over another.
- **Draft Status & Guardrails**: Due to the integration of AI tools in our workflow, all system-generated suggestions must be treated as drafts. The final communication must be reviewed by a human expert. Wagering language is strictly prohibited.

## Addressing Regional Status and Resistance
When communicating the rollout of the new "Four Pillars" Competency Framework (Vision, Entrepreneurship, Passion, Trust), regional managers face specific challenges:
1. **APAC Region**: Highly driven by local market trends. May view Group directives as too slow. Communications should emphasize *Entrepreneurship* and agility.
2. **EMEA Region**: Deeply rooted in historical Brand DNA. May resist homogenization. Communications should emphasize *Trust* and the preservation of individual brand identities.
3. **Americas**: Culturally diverse with variable retention rates. Communications should emphasize *Vision* and *Passion*, focusing on long-term career growth.
