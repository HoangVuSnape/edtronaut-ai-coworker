# Inter-Brand Talent Mobility Policy (Gucci Group)

## 1. Purpose
The Inter-Brand Mobility Policy aims to increase fluid talent exchange between distinct brands within the Gucci Global Group. Driven by the CHRO's mandate, this policy breaks down traditional corporate silos, allowing top talent to share best practices while protecting individual brand DNA.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all management-level employees (Band 3 and above) who have fulfilled at least 24 months in their current role.

## 3. Policy Guidelines
### 3.1. Approvals and Coordination
- To prevent talent drain from high-performing brands, all mobility requests must be approved by the originating Brand HR Director and the Group CHRO.
- The Group CEO reserves the right to veto transfers that threaten the competitive edge of a specific brand unit.

### 3.2. Integration with Competency Framework
- Candidates applying for inter-brand movement must score high in the **Entrepreneurship** and **Trust** categories within their latest performance reviews.
- The receiving brand must provide an orientation that explicitly maps their unique Brand DNA back to the overarching Group Competencies.

### 3.3. Compensation and Benefits
- Employees transferring out of their local region will receive a standard relocation package (the "Gucci Group Global Expat Pack").
- Base compensation will be adjusted according to the local cost of living and the destination brand's specific compensation bands.

## 4. Resistance and Mitigation
We anticipate resistance from regional Brand Directors who may view this program as "poaching" by larger sister brands. 
- **Mitigation Strategy**: The Employer Branding & Internal Communications Manager will launch an internal campaign emphasizing that mobility creates well-rounded leaders who often return to their original brands with amplified skill sets.
