# Gucci Global Group: Core Competency Framework

## Introduction
In our ongoing journey at Gucci 2.0, the HR function is transitioning toward a unified competency framework. This framework is essential for assessing talent, shaping leadership development, and ensuring that our underlying culture remains consistent, even while respecting the unique DNA of individual brands within the group.

The framework consists of four foundational pillars: **Vision, Entrepreneurship, Passion, and Trust**.

---

## 1. Vision
* **Definition**: The ability to anticipate market trends, imagine future states of luxury fashion, and continuously adapt internal processes to meet those futures.
* **Behaviors Expected**:
  - Communicating clear, inspirational goals across brand silos.
  - Making decisions that support the long-term sustainability and prestige of the Group.
  - Fostering a future-proof workplace.
* **Evaluation Metrics**: Strategic planning execution rate, cross-brand initiative participation.

## 2. Entrepreneurship
* **Definition**: Empowering employees to take calculated risks, act with agility, and treat their domain as their own independent business.
* **Behaviors Expected**:
  - Agile problem-solving in fast-paced retail environments.
  - Proposing new initiatives for internal mobility and talent exchange.
  - Responding rapidly to local market nuances without waiting for heavy corporate bureaucracy.
* **Evaluation Metrics**: Number of local initiatives launched, speed to market for new HR policies.

## 3. Passion
* **Definition**: An infectious drive for excellence in luxury goods, customer experience, and colleague support.
* **Behaviors Expected**:
  - Demonstrating strong emotional investment in the brand DNA.
  - Acting as an enthusiastic mentor for junior talent.
  - Elevating the everyday employee experience.
* **Evaluation Metrics**: Peer feedback scores, employee retention rates.

## 4. Trust
* **Definition**: Building psychological safety, operating with utter transparency, and upholding corporate NDAs and ethical standards.
* **Behaviors Expected**:
  - Respecting the autonomy of different brands while executing Group directives.
  - Communicating openly about challenges in rolling out new competency models.
  - Fostering an inclusive environment where all voices are heard.
* **Evaluation Metrics**: Upward feedback, internal audit compliance.
